Performance Metrics Interpretation Guide

Understanding your groundwater system health indicators

Methodology: These metrics follow the Shakhane et al. (2024) approach using standardized z-scores (threshold: -0.5). Each metric evaluates a different aspect of groundwater system performance.

● Reliability (% Satisfactory Performance)

Measures the proportion of time the system performs satisfactorily

Excellent > 80%

Meaning: System performs well most of the time

Action: Maintain current management practices

Good 60-80%

Meaning: Acceptable performance with some stress periods

Action: Monitor trends and optimize where possible

Moderate 40-60%

Meaning: Frequent failures affecting system performance

Action: Implement adaptive management strategies

Poor 20-40%

Meaning: System under significant stress

Action: Urgent intervention required

Critical < 20%

Meaning: Severe system degradation

Action: Emergency response and comprehensive review

● Resilience (Recovery Rate)

Measures how quickly the system recovers from failure events

Fast Recovery > 0.5

Meaning: System recovers quickly (avg < 2 periods)

Action: Good natural resilience - maintain conditions

Moderate Recovery 0.25-0.5

Meaning: Average recovery time 2-4 periods

Action: Monitor recovery patterns, support natural recharge

Slow Recovery 0.1-0.25

Meaning: Recovery takes 4-10 periods

Action: Enhance recharge, reduce extraction during stress

Very Slow < 0.1

Meaning: Extended recovery periods (> 10 periods)

Action: Critical - may indicate aquifer depletion

● Vulnerability (% Relative Severity)

Measures the average severity of failures relative to maximum observed

Low Impact < 40%

Meaning: Failures are relatively mild

Action: Current buffer capacity adequate

Moderate Impact 40-60%

Meaning: Moderate severity when failures occur

Action: Develop contingency plans for drought periods

High Impact 60-80%

Meaning: Failures have significant consequences

Action: Strengthen buffers, diversify water sources

Severe Impact > 80%

Meaning: Critical deficits when system fails

Action: Major infrastructure investment needed

● Sustainability Index (Combined Score)

Combined metric: S = Reliability × Resilience × (1 - Vulnerability)

Sustainable > 0.5

Meaning: Long-term viability assured

Action: System is sustainable - maintain best practices

Moderately Sustainable 0.3-0.5

Meaning: Acceptable but room for improvement

Action: Optimize management for better long-term outlook

At Risk 0.1-0.3

Meaning: Long-term sustainability uncertain

Action: Implement comprehensive management reforms

Unsustainable < 0.1

Meaning: Current trajectory not viable

Action: Emergency intervention - fundamental changes required

💡 Tip: Use filters to view metrics for specific catchments or parameters. For detailed period-by-period analysis, refer to the Failure Analysis table.
